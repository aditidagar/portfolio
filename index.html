Summer research at the Continuum Robotics Laboratory

During the summer of 2021 I had the opportunity to work as a research student at the Continuum Robotics Laboratory under the supervision of Dr. Jessica Burgner-Kahrs and Dr. Quentin Peyron on the contact-aided catheter navigation project.

The project involved designing a model of a tendon-driven continuum robot to be used as a catheter for performing robotic thrombectomies. The model of the continuum robot was based on the Cosserat Rod Theory and leveraged contact with the vessel walls during the maneuvering of the catheter to the clot site. I was involved in implementing the model in C++ as part of designing a simulator for the robot catheter.

Converting the mathematical model of the robot into code relied heavily on numerical methods which were implemented using the Eigen library and the GNU Scientific Library. The model being a boundary value problem was solved using the shooting method. More precisely, the Runge-Kutta 4,5 routine was used to integrate the ordinary differential equations and the levenberg-marquardt method was used to reduce the boundary conditions to zero. The model ultimately yields the catheter position, shape and orientation in the form of a body frame matrix.
